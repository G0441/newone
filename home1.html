Specifications

Engine

The S15 came with the legendary SR20DET 2.0L inline-4 (1998 cc) Turbocharged (intercooler-equipped) Around 250 PS (247 hp) in the Spec-R version Strong aftermarket support and tuning potential There was also a non-turbo version: SR20DE (less powerful, naturally aspirated)

Horsepower

“Hoses” in the S15 typically refer to various fluid and air hoses in the engine bay, such as: Radiator hoses (coolant flow) Intercooler hoses/piping (turbo air intake) Vacuum hoses (boost control, sensors) Fuel lines These are critical for: Cooling the engine Maintaining turbo pressure Proper fuel delivery

Drive Type

Drive type: Rear-Wheel Drive (RWD)-ideal for drifting and sporty handling Power delivery: Turbo kicks in at mid RPM Smooth but strong acceleration for a lightweight coupe

Transmission

1.6-speed manual transmission Found in Spec-R (turbo) Close gear ratios for performance driving 2.4-speed automatic transmission Less common among enthusiasts More comfort-focused

Popular

The "Nissan Silvia S15" is popular because it perfectly blends lightweight agility, rear-wheel-drive balance, and the tunable SR20DET turbo engine, making it a favorite among drifters and car enthusiasts. Its sleek, aerodynamic design, responsive handling, and strong aftermarket support allow owners to easily modify power, suspension, and styling. The S15 became iconic in motorsports and car culture, appearing in drifting competitions, video games, and street scenes, cementing its reputation as one of the most versatile and beloved Japanese sports coupes of its era.
